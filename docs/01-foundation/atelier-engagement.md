## Why "Software Factory" is the right term anyway

> *[Julie writes — the defense. Software Factory is established public-domain vocabulary in software engineering literature; the term predates and is independent of Sankar's critique; DoD/defense ecosystem uses it (Platform One, Iron Bank, software factory accreditation). The factory framing names *operational responsibility for delivery at scale*, which is what SF² is doing — not a claim about how engineers should be organized internally.]*

### Term provenance (D4 §Claim 4)

> *[Julie writes — historical trace of "software factory" as a term, naming who/where/when, so readers can see the term is industrial-lineage, not Julie's invention.]*

- **Bob Bemer, 1968** — first published use of "software factory" framing.
- **Cusumano, Michael, 1991** — *Japan's Software Factories: A Challenge to U.S. Management.* Oxford University Press.
- **Greenfield, Jack and Keith Short, 2004** — *Software Factories: Assembling Applications with Patterns, Models, Frameworks, and Tools.* Wiley.
- **U.S. Department of Defense** — 20+ accredited Software Factories operating today (Platform One, Kessel Run, Kobayashi Maru, Black Pearl, others).

## SF² + Coadaptive as the synthesis

> *[Julie writes — the resolution. Both framings are right at different layers. Factory is right at the *operational accountability* layer (Sec 01 Foundation, Sec 02 Stewardship). Atelier is right at the *creative-act / role-architecture* layer (which SF² does not own and never claimed to). Coadaptive Security extends to the *unit of operation* in the AI era (Ch 03 Unit of Defense) — and that unit is closer to a paired-intelligence cell than to either a factory worker or an artist. The synthesis names the layer at which each framing is load-bearing, instead of asking readers to pick a team.]*

## The AI-era production-model question

> *[Julie writes — the harder question Sankar's framing surfaces and SF² + Coadaptive must answer. In the AI era, the unit of production is shifting from team-of-humans to human-with-agents. Neither factory (human assembly line) nor atelier (lone craftsman + tools) maps cleanly onto a paired-intelligence operating cell. This is the question Coadaptive Security Chapter 03 (The Unit of Defense) takes up. Foundation does not resolve it; it names it.]*
